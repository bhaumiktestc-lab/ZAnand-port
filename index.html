Global Awareness & Ethics

🌍
Global Energy Context

From an engineering perspective, energy access and sustainability are no longer optional concerns. With rising global demand and environmental impact, the shift toward renewable energy and efficient systems is critical. My interest in EVs and solar-based systems comes from this larger context — reducing dependency on fossil fuels and improving energy efficiency at scale.

⚖️
Engineering Ethics

Working on systems like power electronics and fault detection carries real responsibility. Engineers must design for safety, reliability, and compliance — not just performance. This has shaped how I approach every design decision in my projects.

⚠️
Inaccurate fault detection → system failures, safety hazards, and outages affecting communities
🔥
Poorly designed battery systems → fire risks and safety violations in consumer electronics
✅
Responsibility to design systems that are safe, reliable, and standard-compliant — not just efficient
🔋
Personal Reflection

Every project I've worked on — from the power bank's safety protection circuits to the ANN fault detection system validated on a real feeder — has reinforced one idea: the engineer's job doesn't end at making something work. It ends when the system is safe, reliable, and ready for the real world. As someone interested in EVs, renewable energy, and smart grids, I see these ethical considerations as central to what it means to build meaningful technology.
